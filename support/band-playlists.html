Band Playlists

Group songs by mood, gig type, or occasion — ready when you need them.

Creating Playlists

Playlists let you group band songs into themed collections. Create a playlist, give it a name, and add songs from your band library. Reorder songs however you like.

📸Screenshot: Creating a band playlist

Ideas for Playlists

Here are some ways bands use playlists:

  • Openers — High-energy songs to kick off a set
  • Slow Burn — Mellow songs for winding down
  • Crowd Requests — Songs people always ask for
  • New Material — Recently learned songs to work into rotation
  • Acoustic Set — Songs that work unplugged
  • Wedding Gig — Appropriate songs for wedding receptions

Playlists vs. Setlists

Playlists are reusable collections — they're not tied to a specific show. Setlists are built for a particular gig. Think of playlists as your ingredient list and setlists as the recipe for a specific night.

Tip

Use playlists as building blocks when creating setlists. Pull from your Openers playlist for the first song, Crowd Requests for the middle, and Slow Burn for the closer.
