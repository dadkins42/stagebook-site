🎼 Charts

Attach chord charts, lead sheets, and reference documents to your songs. Available in two places for two different purposes.

Why are charts in two places?

You'll find charts in both your personal song library and in the band section. This isn't a mistake — they serve different purposes:

Personal Charts

Charts attached to songs in your personal library are just for you. Use them for your own notes, markings, and reference material. Maybe you have a chart with your specific fingerings, capo position, or performance notes that only matter to you.

📱
Screenshot: Personal chart attached to a song

Band Charts

Charts in the band section are shared with every member. When you attach a chart to a band song, everyone in the band can see it. This is perfect for the "official" version — the chord chart everyone plays from, the arrangement notes, or the lead sheet.

📱
Screenshot: Band chart shared with members
💡 Tip

You might keep a personal chart with your own notes and markings, while also having a clean "official" chart shared with the band. Both can exist for the same song — they're independent.

Adding a Chart

You can add charts from photos, files, or by creating them directly in the app.

From a Photo

Take a photo of a handwritten chart, a page from a book, or anything else. StageBook will attach it to your song for quick reference on stage.

From a File

Import PDF chord charts, lead sheets, or any document you already have. Great for charts you've downloaded or received from other musicians.

📱
Screenshot: Adding a chart to a song

Viewing Charts on Stage

When you're performing, you need quick access to your charts without fumbling through menus. Charts are accessible right from the song view — just tap to open, pinch to zoom, and swipe between multiple charts.
