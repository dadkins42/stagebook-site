📋 Playlists

Organize your band's songs into playlists for any occasion — by mood, genre, gig type, or however you think about your music.

Creating a Playlist

Tap the + button on the Playlists tab to start a new playlist. Give it a name, then add songs from your band library. Drag and drop to put them in the order you want.

Playlists are flexible — the same song can appear in multiple playlists, so you can organize your repertoire in as many ways as you need.

📱
Screenshot: Creating a new playlist

Organizing by Mood or Gig

Create playlists that match different situations your band plays. A high-energy playlist for bar gigs, a mellow one for acoustic sets, a crowd-pleasers list for festivals — whatever works for your band.

When a new gig comes in, you'll already have a head start on building a setlist because your songs are pre-organized into logical groups.

📱
Screenshot: Multiple playlists organized by mood
💡 Tip

Create a "New to the Rotation" playlist for songs you've recently added. It's a handy way to track what needs extra rehearsal time before it's gig-ready.

Using Playlists for Shows

When you're building a setlist for an upcoming show, you can pull songs directly from your playlists. This makes it fast to assemble a set that fits the vibe of the venue or event.

Think of playlists as your building blocks and setlists as the finished product. Playlists help you think about your repertoire; setlists are what you actually play.
